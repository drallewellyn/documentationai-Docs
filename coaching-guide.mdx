## Overview

AdvanceMed coaching is **1-on-1 career support** for doctors, delivered by Dr Anthony Llewellyn. Sessions are **online** by default, with the option of an in-person session in Newcastle, NSW for clients who can travel.

This guide covers the full flow. For the policy detail and the booking link, see the dedicated [Strategy / Clarity Calls](/help-center/services/strategy-clarity-calls) page.

<Callout kind="info">
  Coaching focuses on **your** specific career questions — IMG pathways, application cycles, interview preparation, career pivots, leadership transitions. It is not generic career advice.
</Callout>

## Preparing for your session

The most productive sessions have **3–5 specific questions** prepared in advance. Examples:

- "Should I apply this year or next year?"
- "Which specialty pathway fits my background better — psychiatry or addiction medicine?"
- "How do I respond to this specific selection criterion?"
- "What should I do differently after my unsuccessful interview?"

Less useful: "Tell me about my career options" — too broad to be solved in 30 minutes.

<Expandable title="Advanced preparation checklist">
  - Bring your **current CV** and any application documents you've drafted
  - Have any feedback from past interviews or applications ready
  - Know your **timeline constraints** — when do you need the answer by?
  - Have **measurable success criteria** — what would you need to achieve in this session for it to be worthwhile?
</Expandable>

## The session itself

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Online (default)" icon="video">
    Sessions run on **Zoom**. You'll get the meeting link in your confirmation email.

    Test your audio and video before joining if you haven't used Zoom recently.
    Expect to be on camera — coaching is most effective face-to-face.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="In-person (Newcastle, NSW)" icon="map-pin">
    Available on request. We'll confirm location and parking details when you book.
    Bring printed copies of any documents you'd like to review.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

All sessions are **recorded** so you can review them later — see [Session recordings](/help-center/account-and-billing/session-recordings).
