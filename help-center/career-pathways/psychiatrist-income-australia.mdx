## Do psychiatrists make good money?

Yes. Consultant psychiatrists generally earn at least **$350,000 AUD** if they work full time. Psychiatrists are [5th on the list of top salary earners in Australia](https://advancemed.com.au/blog/salary-of-australian-doctors-surgeons/).

## Consultant psychiatrist earnings

Earnings vary by sector:

- **Public health service** — full-time permanent positions pay around **$350,000 to $450,000 AUD per annum**.
- **Private sector** — substantially higher, with potential earnings reaching **up to $800,000 AUD**.

## Which subspecialty earns the most?

It's hard to say which specific field of psychiatry earns the most.

Some subspecialties are largely confined to public hospital settings — for example **consultation-liaison psychiatry** — so consultants in those areas tend to earn somewhat less than those in the private sector.

Subspecialties that lend themselves best to private-sector work — and therefore have higher earning potential — include:

- **Forensic psychiatry**
- **Adult psychiatry**

## Compared to other mental-health roles

As is generally the case with other medical specialties, **consultant psychiatrists earn significantly more** than other mental-health professionals such as psychiatry trainees, psychologists, social workers and nurses.
