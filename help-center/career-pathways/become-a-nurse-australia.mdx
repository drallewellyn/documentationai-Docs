The first step in pursuing nursing in Australia involves choosing between two pathways:

- **Registered Nurse** — requires a **Bachelor of Nursing**.
- **Enrolled Nurse** — a **Diploma of Nursing** is sufficient.

Both pathways require registration through **AHPRA** (the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency). Once approved, nurses can commence work in general nursing roles.

## For international applicants

International healthcare professionals seeking to practise in Australia face additional requirements beyond the standard credentials. They must obtain the **appropriate visa** alongside meeting the educational and registration standards outlined for domestic applicants.
